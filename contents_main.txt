Backpain 
- loss of bowel or bladder control\n
- numbness, tingling, or weakness in one or both legs\n
- onset following trauma, such as a fall or a blow to the back\n
- intense, constant pain that gets worse at night\n
- presence of unexplained weight loss\n
- pain associated with a throbbing sensation in the abdomen\n
- presence of fever

Earache 
- Sleep problems and restlessness at night\n
- Problems with hearing, more than usual\n
- Loss in appetite\n
- Incessant crying in children\n
- Ear pain\n
- Fever\n
- Dizziness\n
- Problem balancing\n
- Irritability\n
- Coughing and running nose


Headache 
- Dull, aching head pain\n
- Sensation of tightness or pressure across your forehead or on the sides and back of your head\n
- Tenderness on your scalp, neck and shoulder muscles


Stomachache 
- constipation.\n
- diarrhea.\n
- gastroenteritis (stomach flu)\n
- acid reflux (when stomach contents leak backward into the esophagus, causing heartburn and other symptoms)\n
- vomiting.\n
- stress.


toothaceh 
- Tooth pain that may be sharp, throbbing, or constant. In some people, pain results only when pressure is applied to the tooth.\n
- Swelling around the tooth.\n
- Fever or headache.\n
- Foul-tasting drainage from the infected tooth


Sore Thorat
- Fever.\n
- Cough.\n
- Runny nose.\n
- Sneezing.\n
- Body aches.\n
- Headache.\n
- Nausea or vomiting.

asthma
- wheezing\n
- breathlessness\n
- chest tightness\n
- coughing\n
- difficulty speaking.



Bronchiolitis
- Vomiting\n
- Audible wheezing sounds\n
- Breathing very fast — more than 60 breaths a minute (tachypnea)  — and shallowly\n
- Labored breathing — the  ribs seem to suck inward when infant inhales\n
- Sluggish or lethargic appearance\n
- Refusal to drink enough, or breathing too fast to eat or drink\n
- Skin turning blue, especially the lips and fingernails (cyanosis)

Pneumonia
- Chest pain when you breathe or cough\n
- Confusion or changes in mental awareness (in adults age 65 and older)\n
- Cough, which may produce phlegm\n
- Fatigue\n
- Fever, sweating and shaking chills\n
- Lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems)\n
  Nausea, vomiting or diarrhea\n
- Shortness of breath


cold & Cough
- Runny or stuffy nose.\n
- Sore throat.\n
- Cough.\n
- Congestion.\n
- Slight body aches or a mild headache.\n
- Sneezing.\n
- Low-grade fever.\n
- Generally feeling unwell (malaise)


Fever
- Sweating\n
- Chills and shivering\n
- Headache\n
- Muscle aches\n
- Loss of appetite\n
- Irritability\n
- Dehydration\n
- General weakness.

Sunburn
- fever\n
- chills\n
- nausea and vomiting\n
- weakness\n
- low blood pressure\n
- fainting\n
- extreme weakness

Rash
- Itchiness.\n
- Skin redness.\n
- Dry, scaly, or crusted skin that might become thick and leathery from long-term scratching.\n
- Formation of small, fluid-filled blisters that might ooze when scratched.\n
- Infection of the areas where the skin has been broken.

acne
- Whiteheads (closed plugged pores)\n
- Blackheads (open plugged pores)\n
- Small red, tender bumps (papules)\n
- Pimples (pustules), which are papules with pus at their tips\n
- Large, solid, painful lumps beneath the surface of the skin (nodules)\n
- Painful, pus-filled lumps beneath the surface of the skin (cystic lesions)
